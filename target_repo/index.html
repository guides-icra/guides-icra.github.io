GUIDES: Enhancing Pre-trained Robot Policies with Semantic Guidance

GUIDES demonstrates how pre-trained robot policies can be enhanced with semantic guidance from foundation models without requiring architectural redesign. The framework employs instructor-distilled embeddings to improve task performance and generalization in robotic manipulation tasks.

Abstract

Pre-trained robot policies serve as the foundation of many validated robotic systems, which encapsulate extensive embodied knowledge. However, they often lack the semantic awareness characteristic of foundation models, and replacing them entirely is impractical in many situations due to high costs and the loss of accumulated knowledge. To address this gap, we introduce GUIDES, a lightweight framework that augments pre-trained policies with semantic guidance from foundation models without requiring architectural redesign.

GUIDES employs a fine-tuned vision-language model (Instructor) to generate contextual instructions, which are encoded by an auxiliary module into guidance embeddings. These embeddings are injected into the policy's latent space, allowing the legacy model to adapt to this new semantic input through brief, targeted fine-tuning. For inference-time robustness, a large language model-based Reflector monitors the Instructor's confidence and, when confidence is low, initiates a reasoning loop that analyzes execution history, retrieves relevant examples, and augments the VLM's context to refine subsequent actions.

Extensive validation in the RoboCasa simulation environment across diverse policy architectures shows consistent and substantial improvements in task success rates. Real-world deployment on a UR5 robot further demonstrates that GUIDES enhances motion precision for critical sub-tasks such as grasping.

The Pre-trained Policy Enhancement Challenge

The challenge with pre-trained robot policies: Pre-trained robot policies encapsulate extensive embodied knowledge and serve as the foundation of many validated robotic systems. However, they often lack the semantic awareness characteristic of foundation models. Replacing them entirely is impractical due to high costs and the loss of accumulated knowledge.

GUIDES approach: GUIDES addresses this challenge by augmenting pre-trained policies with semantic guidance from foundation models without requiring architectural redesign. The framework employs instructor-distilled embeddings that are injected into the policy's latent space, enabling the legacy model to adapt to semantic input through targeted fine-tuning.

Key innovation: Rather than replacing existing policies, GUIDES provides a lightweight pathway to upgrade them with semantic understanding while preserving their accumulated knowledge and validation history.

GUIDES Framework Architecture

GUIDES framework consists of three key components:

  • Instructor: A fine-tuned vision-language model that generates contextual instructions based on visual observations
  • Guidance Embeddings: An auxiliary module that encodes instructions into embeddings that are injected into the policy's latent space
  • Reflector: A large language model-based component that monitors confidence and initiates reasoning loops when needed

The framework allows pre-trained policies to adapt to semantic input through brief, targeted fine-tuning while preserving their original capabilities.

Experimental Results

GUIDES demonstrates consistent and substantial improvements in task success rates across diverse policy architectures in the RoboCasa simulation environment. The framework shows robust performance enhancements while maintaining the efficiency of pre-trained policies.

Real-World Deployment

Real-world deployment on a UR5 robot demonstrates that GUIDES enhances motion precision for critical sub-tasks such as grasping, validating the framework's practical applicability beyond simulation environments.

Framework Efficiency

GUIDES provides a lightweight and resource-efficient pathway to upgrade pre-trained robot policies. The framework requires only brief, targeted fine-tuning while preserving the accumulated knowledge and validation history of existing policies.
